1. Executive Summary

AFM (Africans First Mesh) is a community-run wireless network that helps neighborhoods bootstrap reliable internet access using affordable, off-grid hardware kits and a token economy aligned to real usage. We adapt proven coverage incentive mechanisms from the DePIN space and re-implement them natively on Algorand using Algorand Standard Assets (ASAs), oracles, and lightweight smart contracts.

The Problem

2.9 Billion

People globally lack internet access, with the majority in Africa

Traditional ISPs won't serve rural and peri-urban communities due to low population density and limited purchasing power. Existing solutions (satellite, mobile data) are either too expensive ($50-100/month) or unreliable.

The Solution

AFM creates a cooperative WiFi network where:

  • Operators deploy solar-powered hotspots with satellite backhaul (Starlink Mini)
  • Users pay for data using stable-priced Data Credits (DC-ASA)
  • Community owns the network through democratic governance
  • Token economics reward coverage and usage, not speculation

8. Governance

AFM uses a hybrid governance model that balances cooperative principles (one-member-one-vote) with token-weighted decisions for treasury management.

Governance Structure

Layer 1: Operational Governance

Voting: One Member = One Vote

Decisions: Network operations, operator policies, coverage expansion, service quality standards

Who Votes: All cooperative members (operators + committed users)

Layer 2: Treasury Governance

Voting: Token-weighted (AFM holdings)

Decisions: Treasury allocations, AFM emissions, economic parameters, presale terms

Who Votes: AFM token holders

Layer 3: Emergency Actions

Voting: Multisig (Board of Directors)

Decisions: Security incidents, smart contract upgrades, regulatory compliance

Who Votes: 5-member elected board (2-year terms)

Governance Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 0-6)

  • Core team manages operations
  • Community feedback via Discord/Telegram
  • Operator onboarding and training

Phase 2: Progressive Decentralization (Months 6-18)

  • Operator council formed (advisory)
  • Community votes on minor decisions
  • Treasury multisig established

Phase 3: Full Cooperative (Month 18+)

  • Formal cooperative incorporation
  • Elected board of directors
  • Full on-chain governance

12. Risks & Mitigations

‚ö†Ô∏è Regulatory Risk

Risk: Crypto regulations may change, affecting token operations

Mitigation: DC are utility tokens (not securities), legal counsel in each jurisdiction, compliance-first approach

‚ö†Ô∏è Technical Risk

Risk: Smart contract bugs, blockchain failures

Mitigation: Audited smart contracts, gradual rollout, emergency pause mechanisms

‚ö†Ô∏è Adoption Risk

Risk: Not enough operators or users join

Mitigation: Generous presale rewards, $0 operator financing, partnerships with local cooperatives

‚ö†Ô∏è Economic Risk

Risk: Token price volatility, burn-mint imbalance

Mitigation: Soulbound DC, stable pricing, treasury reserves, community governance

‚ö†Ô∏è Infrastructure Risk

Risk: Starlink service disruptions, solar equipment failures

Mitigation: Diversified backhaul options, operator training, equipment insurance fund

‚ö†Ô∏è Competition Risk

Risk: Traditional ISPs or other DePIN networks compete

Mitigation: Cooperative advantages (no profit extraction), community ownership, off-grid capability
